SBU Rocket Team for NASA's Student Launch Initiative

This project is still ongoing.

This project is affilated with the SBU Rocket Team, which is a part of the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics (AIAA) chapter at Stony Brook University.

Problem

Altitude Precision: Achieve the declared altitude of 5,000 feet while maintaining a stable flight profile.
Payload Integration: Safely transport the STEMNaut payload and gather quality telemetry data throughout the flight.
Competition Requirements: Comply with NASA's guidelines for scoring, which include rocket design, report quality, data accuracy, and altitude achievement.

Technology Used

RockSim: Modeled the rocket's performance and validated stability margins through iterative flight simulations.
3D Printing: Fabricated custom components for electronics mounts and the STEMNaut payload to reduce weight and streamline assembly.
Machine Shop & Power Tools: Constructed subscale rocket sections and fins under strict safety protocols; handled propellants with licensed professionals.

What I learned:

Leadership & Decision-Making: When the subscale rocket build stalled due to indecision, I took the initiative to finalize design choices and push the project forward. This experience taught me the importance of stepping up as a leader while balancing input from teammates.
Risk Management & Adaptability Under Pressure: When a critical design near-miss with the fins threatened the subscale rocket's stability, we had just two days before launch—no time to remake parts. I led a rapid, high-stakes assessment of the compromised stability margin, carefully balancing the risks of flying with imperfect fins against the time and materials constraints. Despite a slight wobble in flight, the subscale launched successfully, reinforcing the importance of thorough risk evaluation, decisive leadership under pressure, and the willingness to adapt plans on tight timelines.
Collaboration & Communication: Working with NASA specialists, faculty advisors, and a large, diverse team helped me develop stronger communication skills. I learned to clearly articulate design rationale and listen to concerns from colleagues.

Looking ahead:

Further Stabilization: Refine fin design and overall stability margin for the full-scale launch in May.
Enhanced Team Dynamics: Establish clearer accountability and leadership structures to avoid delays.
Data Integration: Improve telemetry systems for real-time tracking and post-flight analysis.

Final Note:

The full-scale rocket is scheduled for launch in May in Alabama, where we will demonstrate the final design's performance and collect comprehensive flight data. This project has already provided invaluable hands-on experience in aerospace engineering, team leadership, and practical manufacturing processes—skills that I intend to carry forward in future endeavors.
