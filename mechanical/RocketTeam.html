Our team successfully completed NASA's prestigious Student Launch Initiative, achieving 24th place out of 53 teams with our 105-inch, 21-pound rocket that reached 4,700 feet altitude.

Key Learnings

Leadership & Cross-Team Coordination

As the vehicle team's motor selection and RockSim specialist, I stepped up to coordinate launch day assembly and inter-team communication. When team coordination was needed, I proactively took charge to ensure everything was properly organized and executed.

Technical Problem-Solving

Extended support to the payload team by helping with sensor data compilation, fixing memory leaks, and implementing multithreading with mutex for global variable access. This cross-functional collaboration was crucial for successful telemetry system integration.

Large-Scale Team Dynamics

Working across multiple teams taught me how to collaborate effectively in large groups while knowing when to step up and make meaningful contributions. I learned to balance supporting others with taking initiative when the project needed decisive action.
