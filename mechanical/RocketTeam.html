SBU Rocket Team

Completed

NASA Student Launch Initiative - Designing, manufacturing, and testing a rocket to reach 5,000 feet altitude

Project Overview

Part of the SBU Rocket Team, affiliated with the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics (AIAA) chapter at Stony Brook University. Our team is competing in NASA's prestigious Student Launch Initiative, designing a rocket system capable of reaching precisely 5,000 feet while carrying scientific payloads.

Technical Challenges

🎯 Altitude Precision

Achieve the declared altitude of 5,000 feet while maintaining a stable flight profile.

📡 Payload Integration

Safely transport the STEMNaut payload and gather quality telemetry data throughout the flight.

📋 Competition Requirements

Comply with NASA's guidelines for scoring, including rocket design, report quality, data accuracy, and altitude achievement.

Key Learnings

Leadership & Decision-Making

When the subscale rocket build stalled due to indecision, I took the initiative to finalize design choices and push the project forward. This experience taught me the importance of stepping up as a leader while balancing input from teammates.

Risk Management & Adaptability

When a critical design near-miss with the fins threatened stability with just two days before launch, I led a rapid assessment of the compromised stability margin. Despite a slight wobble in flight, the subscale launched successfully, reinforcing the importance of thorough risk evaluation and decisive leadership under pressure.

Collaboration & Communication

Working with NASA specialists, faculty advisors, and a large, diverse team helped me develop stronger communication skills. I learned to clearly articulate design rationale and listen to concerns from colleagues.

Looking Ahead

🎯 Further Stabilization

Refine fin design and overall stability margin for the full-scale launch in May.

👥 Enhanced Team Dynamics

Establish clearer accountability and leadership structures to avoid delays.

📊 Data Integration

Improve telemetry systems for real-time tracking and post-flight analysis.

Mission Complete

The full-scale rocket was successfully launched in Alabama, where we demonstrated the final design's performance and collected comprehensive flight data. This project provided invaluable hands-on experience in aerospace engineering, team leadership, and practical manufacturing processes.
